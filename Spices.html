Spices of India

Tumeric

Origin: Southern India

Turmeric is a vibrant yellow spice, known for its earthy flavor and anti-inflammatory properties, widely used in cooking and medicine.

Cardamom

Origin: Kerala, India

Cardamom is an aromatic spice with a sweet, floral flavor, often used in cooking, baking, and traditional medicine for its digestive benefits.

Cumin

Origin: North India

Cumin is a warm, earthy spice with a slightly nutty flavor, commonly used in cooking for its rich, aromatic qualities.

Garam Masala

Origin: North India

Garam masala is a fragrant blend of spices, including cumin, coriander, cardamom, and cloves, used in Indian cuisine for its warm, complex flavor.

Cloves

Origin: Kerala, India

Cloves are aromatic flower buds with a sweet, spicy flavor, commonly used in cooking, baking, and traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Indian Red Chili

Origin: Andhra Pradesh, India

Indian red chili is a fiery spice known for its intense heat and vibrant color, widely used in Indian cuisine to add spice and flavor.
